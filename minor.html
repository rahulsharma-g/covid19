Elaboration about our AI based Chest Diagnosis

Recently, A new disease that occurred in 2019 and that was not known previously in humans was coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19). Coronaviruses (CoVs) are a wide variety of viruses that cause respiratory diseases such as severe acute respiratory syndrome (SARS-CoV) and Middle East respiratory syndrome (MERS-CoV). The new coronavirus started to spread in China in December 2019 and later spread to many other countries. Deep learning is one of the most helpful methods of artificial intelligence for detecting COVID-19 infections from medical images, such as X-rays, especially when a limited image dataset is accessible. The first screening procedure is a chest X-ray. It plays a major role in COVID-19 diagnosis. COVID-19 treatment is usually related both to pneumonia symptoms and to chest rays. Chest X-rays have become the first imaging tool to play a significant role. Recently, widely accessible X-rays have not only improved in stable cases but also improved in patients with COVID-19.

Data collection methods will be employed on several studies based on X-Rays, including analyses of operating methods and accuracy in detecting infection from chest images, particularly COVID-19. Detection can be based Deep Learning of Artificial Intelligence into a software/Tool to differentiate between COVID-19 and other chest infections like Tuberculosis. For the training dataset, 103 CXR images of COVID-19 were downloaded from GitHub COVID-19 chest x-ray dataset. Five hundred images of non-COVID-19 pneumonia and 500 images of the normal lung were downloaded from the Kaggle RSNA Pneumonia Detection Challenge dataset. To balance the dataset, we expanded the COVID-19 dataset to 500 images by slight rotation (probability = 1, max rotation = 5) and zooming (probability = 0.5, percentage area = 0.9) of the original images using the Augmentor Python package.
